## The AI deployment gap

Today the world of AI is rapidly evolving. There is cutting edge research being published by research groups consistently. If you're an avid [Arxiv](https://arxiv.org) reader, you know exactly what I am talking about. There are [107 unique publications](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/recent?skip=0&show=50) published just today in the AI category. Let that sink in. 

Moreover, the amount of amazing demos we're seeing on platforms like [ùïè](https://x.com/search?q=introducing%20AI&src=typed_query) and [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/search/results/content/?keywords=introducing%20AI&origin=SWITCH_SEARCH_VERTICAL&sid=\(Qf) is uncounted for. The advent of coding formats like "[vibe coding](https://x.com/karpathy/status/1886192184808149383)" have further increased the number of demos we see on a regular basis. But how many AI tools are we actually using? Not a lot. The gap between an amazing demo and a product that works in production still remains significant.

Why does this gap exist? That's a topic for another day, but we see the same thread of problems with AI demos: reliability challenges, scale and performance issues, integration complexity, data knowledge gaps, and data flywheel challenges. These challenges create significant barriers for companies seeking to harness AI's potential. Without specialized expertise, organizations either settle for suboptimal implementations or abandon promising AI initiatives altogether. This is where Rubric steps in.

## What is an applied AI lab?

It's helpful to first define what an applied AI lab is. A traditional AI lab is associated with research and model providers come to mind, such as [OpenAI](https://openai.com), [Anthropic](https://www.anthropic.com), etc. Whereas an applied AI lab is focused on building products. The focus is geared towards the application layer versus the model layer. 

As an applied AI lab, we operate at the intersection of cutting-edge AI research and practical implementation. We combine research-backed methodologies with hands-on development expertise to create AI solutions that work reliably in real-world environments.

3. **Problem-first thinking**:  

Focusing on solving genuine business challenges rather than deploying technology for its own sake
